Projects

Research projects and current directions.

This page groups the main empirical projects and datasets under the broader frame of Flourishing in Context.

Positive mental health

Positive Mental Health in Adolescents

Examines the structure of adolescent well-being, strengths, resilience, self-esteem, emotional functioning, and social connectedness. This line of work asks what positive mental health looks like when studied as a system of functioning rather than as the absence of distress.

Resilience

Resilience across cohorts and contexts

Studies how adolescent resilience changes across developmental stage, historical period, and sociocultural setting, with attention to both stable domains and context-sensitive features.

Social norms

Self, peers, and perceived norms

Investigates how adolescents perceive themselves relative to the average peer, and whether perceived self-peer gaps are linked with well-being, stress, depressive symptoms, and self-concept clarity.

Digital environments

Identity in hybrid social worlds

Examines adolescent identity development in a world where online and offline social interactions are increasingly intertwined, especially around peer influence, comparison, belonging, and self-presentation.

Common thread

From environments to self-understanding.

Across projects, the central question is how social worlds shape adolescents' understanding of themselves and their well-being.

The empirical focus varies across datasets and projects, but the theoretical focus remains consistent: positive mental health develops through the interaction between individual functioning, social context, and social cognition.
