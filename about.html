About

Studying adolescent mental health as a social and developmental phenomenon.

I am a social psychologist whose work examines how adolescents develop well-being, resilience, identity, and self-understanding within the social worlds around them.

Why positive matters

Mental health is often discussed as if it were simply the opposite of mental illness. That framing is useful for identifying distress, but it is incomplete for understanding how young people develop, adapt, relate, and thrive.

My work focuses on positive mental health in adolescence, including well-being, resilience, identity, social connectedness, self-esteem, and psychological strengths. The aim is not to ignore distress, but to understand the full range of adolescent functioning.

Why context matters

I approach adolescent mental health from a social psychological perspective. Where much of psychology focuses on the individual, my work examines the social contexts that surround them.

Families, peers, schools, communities, digital environments, and broader cultural structures all shape how adolescents think, feel, and function. This perspective draws on ecological approaches to development, where young people are understood as embedded within nested layers of social influence.

Why social cognition matters

What interests me most is mechanism: how do social environments become psychologically meaningful?

My research examines social cognition as a pathway through which contextual factors translate into individual well-being. This includes self-perception, perceived peer norms, social comparison, self-concept clarity, identity, intersubjectivity, and status beliefs.

Adolescence is a developmental period when young people are especially responsive to their social worlds. That makes it both a period of vulnerability and a period of opportunity.

Research vision

Flourishing in Context brings these strands together. It asks how adolescents develop positive mental health within the environments they inhabit, and how research can better identify the social conditions that support young people before problems become entrenched.
